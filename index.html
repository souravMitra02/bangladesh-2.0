Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and economist, is celebrated for pioneering microfinance and founding Grameen Bank. Born on June 28, 1940, he introduced small loans without collateral to empower impoverished people, especially women, to start self-sustaining businesses. His innovative approach lifted countless individuals out of poverty and inspired global financial models for economic development. Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006, Dr. Yunus continues to advocate for social business and sustainable development.
